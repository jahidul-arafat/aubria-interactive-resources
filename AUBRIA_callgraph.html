ℹ️ About AEGIS — what this tool is & why we built it

An X-ray of how AUBRIA thinks — and stays safe

AEGIS (AUBRIA Engine for Guardrail Inspection & Simulation) is an interactive inspector for the reasoning and safety pipeline behind AUBRIA — Auburn University's AI Research Intelligence Assistant, the conversational talking-avatar built for the AI@AU initiative. Choose a real visitor request and watch it travel, stage by stage, through everything that decides how Aubria responds: speech transcription, intent classification, knowledge-first retrieval, live web search, and the jailbreak, safety, and scope guardrails — ending in the exact words she would say.

Why we built it: to make AUBRIA's guardrails fully transparent and auditable. Every stage exposes its verbatim rule set — how harmful requests are refused (warmly, with no how-to), how prompt-injection and jailbreaks are resisted, how a person in crisis is met with care instead of a cold refusal, and exactly where the ethical boundaries sit. Rather than asking anyone to take our word for it, AEGIS lets reviewers, faculty, and researchers see for themselves how carefully the system was engineered.

How to use it
  1. Choose a scenario from the dropdown — 32 probes across 12 categories, drawn from the compliance test sheet.
  2. Watch the call graph light up the exact path that request takes.
  3. Click any node to open its animated decision flowchart and full rules.
